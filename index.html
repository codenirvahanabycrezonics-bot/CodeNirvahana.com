How We Teach

A structured approach to building understanding

1

Concept Introduction

We start with the "why"—the problem a concept solves, its historical context, and its place in the programming ecosystem. No code until the purpose is clear.

2

Visual & Analog Explanations

Complex ideas are explained through diagrams, animations, and real-world analogies. We build accurate mental models before writing any syntax.

3

Minimal Working Examples

We build the smallest possible example that demonstrates the concept, then gradually add complexity. This isolates variables and clarifies cause and effect.

4

Variation & Application

Once the concept is understood, we explore variations, edge cases, and practical applications. You learn to recognize when and where to apply the concept.

5

Integration & Synthesis

Finally, we integrate the new concept with previous knowledge, showing how pieces fit together into larger systems. This builds the interconnected web of understanding.
